Natural Calamities

Disasters — like earthquakes, war, wildfires, or severe weather events — can happen close to home or far away. But when we hear the news, it affects us. We feel for the people who might be hurt and the communities that are damaged. It has a deeper impact if you know people who are affected.
Depending on the situation, a relief group may need donations of food, water, or other supplies. They may need volunteers for a clean-up project, or to set up homes for refugees, or to pack boxes of food or supplies. Often, raising money helps the most.

By giving to charity, you may well be helping to remind friends
and family of causes they themselves are passionate about and would like to support.

Your charitable donations could even bring about a family-wide effort
to back a charity or charities that have special significance to you as a group.

Giving benefits society's neediest and most vulnerable.
Often the people in society who are in need are the most overlooked

Blood Donation

Blood donation is a voluntary procedure that can help save lives. There are several types of blood donation. Each type helps meet different medical needs.

Women Empowernment

The donation is for supporting women empowerment. Your contribution will be used to provide opportunities to women, such as funding for education and entrepreneurship initiatives.

Orphanage Donation

Education is the root cause of your donation then sponsor the child education through a cash donation or book donation.

Clothes Donation

By donating your unwanted clothes to charity, you're not only helping the environment, you're also supporting brilliant causes.

Organ Donation

Organ donation takes healthy organs and tissues from one person for transplantation into another. Experts say that the organs from one donor can save or help as many as 50 people.

Animal Aid Donation

Street animals need your help. Your donation ensures that injured and ill ownerless animals are rescued and receive life-saving medical attention.
